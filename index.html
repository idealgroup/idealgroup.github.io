Who We Are

Through the power of mathematics, we aim to ensure that the freedom to transact is upheld for all individuals globally, recognizing that free and fair access to money is the foundation upon which all other human liberties are secured.

Our work centers on the development of private and permissionless Bitcoin scaling technologies, including the advancement of BitVM and its underlying cryptographic primitives.

We work toward { ideal } Bitcoin—not good-enough Bitcoin—as a principled undertaking, and welcome partners unintimidated by this quixotic vision.

Team

Robin Linus

Robin is the creator of BitVM, and it’s more recent BitVM2 and BitVM3 updates. He is currently a PhD student at Stanford studying Bitcoin with a focus on bridging protocols. Robin leads the BitVM Alliance creating the first mainnet-ready BitVM implemetation, which is used in some form by all existing BitVM and Garbled Circuits based bridges.

Liam Eagen

Liam is the former chief scientist and founding cryptographer of Alpen Labs. He worked on BitVM2 and the Groth16 verifier (GLOCKS?) for these protocols and co-discovered the use of Garbled Circuits for CDS on Bitcoin. He previously worked on SNARK research for companies including Aztec, Polygon Zero, and Blockstream.

Ying Tong Lai

Ying Tong is a senior cryptography engineer with previous work at ZCash and the Ethereum Foundation. She is one of the primary authors Halo2 with extensive experience implementing circuits and proof systems. Ying Tong is the primary author of the Argo Garbled Circuits implementation, the industry-leading scheme for BitVM3.
